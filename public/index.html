Alvin Lungu

Contemporary Painter â€¢ Ghana

About the Artist

Born in 1996, Alvin Lungu, is a Zambian multidisciplinary visual artist working in different artistic mediums, such as painting, sculpture, installation to express his creative vision. His work may involve a diverse range of materials and techniques, reflecting their exploration of cultural themes. He often explores the concept of cultural identity, examining the ways in which individuals and communities define themselves within a broader cultural context. Alvin draws his inspiration from his own heritage, traditions, and experiences, as well as explore the intersections of different cultures and their influences. Rites of passage are also a central theme in his work. Alvin explore rituals and ceremonies associated with important life transitions, such as birth, coming of age, marriage, or death. Through his artistic expression, he tries capture the symbolism, emotions, and significance of these rites, shedding light on their cultural and spiritual dimensions. Ancestral spirituality is another prominent theme in Lungu s work. He is mainly inspired from indigenous or traditional belief systems, myths, folklore, or ancestral wisdom to explore the connection between the past and present. Through his artistic creations, Alvin seeks to honor and preserve ancestral knowledge, highlight spiritual practices, and invite reflection on our relationship with the past and its influence on our lives.

Style & Technique

Bold color palettes, Palette knife Work and Focus on people.

About the Artist

Daudi Yves Magisha was born in 2000. Daudi Yves Magisha is a contemporary artist living and working in Zambia. He explores the link between mental health and creativity, as well as toxic masculinity and menâ€™s emotions, using art as a means of self-reflection and a tool for change and inspiration. Through innovative conceptual drawings and paintings on paper and canvas, Daudi engages in multidisciplinary modes of art to address and find solutions to common issues affecting local and global communities. In his work, he uses painting and mixed media to explore themes of change, self-reflection, and enlightenment, drawing inspiration from the Masai tribe. Incorporating symbolic forms like open and closed boxes to represent open-mindedness and closed-mindedness, he challenges viewers to broaden their perspectives and treat themselves and others with compassion. His art addresses diverse topics such as growth, mental health, and societal issues, aiming to push viewers beyond their comfort zones and inspire positive change.
